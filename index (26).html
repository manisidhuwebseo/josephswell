Joseph's Well: Turn the Air Around You Into Clean Drinking Water

Joseph's Well is a step-by-step home guide that shows you how to build a simple device that pulls clean drinking water straight out of the air around you. The science behind it is called atmospheric water generation, but you do not need to understand the science to use it. You just need to follow the instructions.

Here is the basic idea in plain words. Air always holds some amount of moisture, even in places that feel dry. Joseph's Well cools that air down until the moisture turns into water droplets, the same way a cold glass of lemonade sweats on a hot day. How Does Joseph's Well Work?

Here is how Joseph's Well actually works, broken into three easy steps. Step one: a small fan pulls in the air around you. Step two: that air passes over a cool surface, which makes the moisture in it turn into water droplets, the same way water forms on a cold drink on a hot day. Step three: those droplets are collected and run through a filter, so what comes out the other end is clean. There is also an optional step where you can add minerals back in, which helps the water taste better and stay healthier to drink.

You will not find expensive machinery here. Commercial water-from-air machines can run into the thousands of dollars. Joseph's Well is built from simple parts you can buy at a regular hardware store, usually for under $150 total.
